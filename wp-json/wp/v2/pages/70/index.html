WHERE AND HOW DID CFC BEGIN?<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

CFC began in 1981 in Manila, Philippines, when Ang Ligaya ng Panginoon<\/em> (Joy in the Lord), a covenanted Christian charismatic community, realized that to effectively renew society, a conversion of both spouses, and consequently families, to Christ was necessary.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The method used consisted of bringing together a small group of prospective couples in a private home. There they were brought to a living relationship with Jesus Christ and to renewal in the power of the Holy Spirit through a series of weekly informal discussions of the gospel in a social environment.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Beginning from the first 16 couples who joined the weekly gathering, CFC became a Christian family life renewal which was later made available to parishes and groups of married couples who wished to live out their Christian life in an active supportive relationship with one another.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The growth of CFC stemmed from its heart for mission and evangelization and its commitment to sharing God\u2019s love with others, beginning from the basic unit \u2013 the family.  It did not take long for CFC to realize that while a couple renewal was essential, it would be incomplete without family renewal.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Through the years, CFC has blossomed into a worldwide ministry, becoming a major force for the renewal of the Christian family life and also of the church.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

WHY SUCH A MINISTRY?<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

CFC wants to rise in defense of the family, which is God\u62af work. CFC wants to bring God\u62af strength and light to those who are struggling to be truly Christian families in the modern world.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

WHO CAN BECOME MEMBERS OF CFC?<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Any validly married Catholic couple can become members of CFC. Although a Catholic movement\/organization, CFC remains open to having non-Catholic Christians as members. CFC draws its membership from all sectors of society, young and old, rich and poor, etc. CFC transcends all cultural, economic, educational, social and other distinctions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

RECOGNITION<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

In 1996, CFC was approved by the Catholic Bishop\u2019s Conference of the Philippines as a national private association of lay faithful.  In 2000, CFC was recognized by the Holy See (Vatican) as a private international association of the lay faithful of pontifical right.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
